The favorable factors for the formation of an ionic bond would be -

1. Low ionization enthalpy, High electron gain enthalpy, High lattice energy.

2. High ionization enthalpy, High electron gain enthalpy, High lattice energy.

3. Low ionization enthalpy, Low electron gain enthalpy, Low lattice energy.

4. High ionization enthalpy, Low electron gain enthalpy, High lattice energy.
Ans. 1
Sol.
HINT: Ionic bonding involves the complete transfer of valence electron(s) between atoms.
 
Explanation:

The formation of ionic bonds depends upon the ease with which neutral atoms can lose or gain electrons. The bond formation also depends upon the lattice energy of the compound formed.

Hence, favorable factors for ionic bond formation are as follows:

(i) Low ionization enthalpy of the metal atom.

(ii) High electron gain enthalpy (Δeg H)∆eg H of a non-metal atom.

(iii) High lattice energy of the compound formed.

106.

Incorrect statement regarding resonating structures is : 

1. The contributing structure must have the same number of unpaired electrons.

2. The contributing structures should have similar energies.

3. The resonance hybrid should have higher energy than any of the contributing structures.

4. None of the above.
Ans. 3
Sol.

HINT:  The resonance hybrid should have lower energy than any of the contributing structure.

Explanation:

Conditions of resonance are: 

1. The relative position of all the atoms must be same in each resonating structures.

2. Number of paired and unpaired electrons in each resonating structure must be same so that there is a continuous change from one bond type to another bond  type may occur.

3. Contributing structures should have almost equal energy .

4. Like charges should be greatly separated and unlikely charges should be placed together.

5. The more electropositive element should preferably have a positive charge and more electronegative element should have a negative .
 
6. The resonance hybrid should have lower energy than any of the contributing structure.

1. O—H‐‐‐SO—H‐‐‐S

3. F—H‐‐‐FF—H‐‐‐F

4. F—H‐‐‐OF—H‐‐‐O

Sol.

HINT: HF has the strongest H-bonding

Explanation:

 H—H—bonding order: ‐‐‐H—F >‐‐‐H—O>‐‐‐H—N‐‐‐H—F >‐‐‐H—O>‐‐‐H—N
