Ans. 2
Sol. HINT: First ionisation potential of Be > B

Explanation: 

1. On going down the group , the electronegativity of the central atom decreases , and the bond between the central atom and hydrogen becomes less polar , so the bond strength also decreases hence it becomes easier to lose a hydrogen atom thus increasing the acidity.

Thus, acidic character decreases as we move from NH3  to AsH3.

2. Across a period the ionization potential increases from left to right due to increase in net nuclear charge of elements.

So it should be like C > B > Be > Li. But there is an exception in the case of Be and B the ionization potential of Be is higher than that of B.

It is because of the fact that electronic configuration of Be is 1s22s2 and that of B is 1s22s22p1 and as electron in p can be removed easily as compared to the electron from s in Be. This is because of fully filled s orbitals.

So less energy will be required in the case of B than in Be. So the Ionization potential of Be will be greater than that of B.

Thus , the correct order will be like C > Be > B > Li instead of like C > B > Be > Li.

3.On moving along a period, the tendency to lose electrons decreases and thus, the metallic character decreases. While on moving down the group, basic character increases due to its increase in metallic character.

So, the basic character of oxide is : Al2O3 < MgO < Na2O < K2O

4. Down the group ionic radius increases. So the correct order of ionic radius is : Li+ < Na+ < K+ < Cs+
