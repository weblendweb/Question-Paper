4.

The energy-releasing metabolic process in which substrate is oxidised without an external electron acceptor is called

1. glycolysis

2. fermentation

3. aerobic respiration

4. photorespiration
Ans. 2
Sol.

In fermentation, the incomplete oxidation of glucose is achieved under anaerobic conditions by set of reactions, where pyruvic acid is converted to CO2 and ethanol. The enzyme pyruvic acid, decarboxylase and alcohol dehydrogenase catalyse these reactions. In animal cells also, like muscles during exercise, where oxygen is inadequate for cellular respiration, pyruvic acid is reduced to lactic acid by lactate dehydrogenase. The reducing agent is NADH + H+, which is reoxidised to NAD+, in both the processes.

12.

Vernalization is the process of

1. flower induction by high temperature treatment

2. inhabitation of flowering by low temperature treatment

3. flower induction by light treatment

4. acceleration of the ability of a plant to produce flowers by chilling treatment
Ans. 4
Sol.

PAGE 252, XI NCERT

Vernalization is the low temperature treatment given to water soaked seeds, slightly germinated seeds or seedling to hasten the time of flowering of plants. CHouard (1960) defined vernalization as the "acquisition or acceleration of the ability to flower by a chilling treatment". The implication is not that genetic of variety is changed to summer or spring from but only that the variety is made to act like the summer and spring form in response to an artificial cold treatment.
